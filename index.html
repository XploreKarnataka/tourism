About Karnataka

Karnataka is the largest state in South India and sixth largest in India. The state is in the south western region of India. It was formed on 1 November 1956.

Karnataka is bordered by the Arabian Sea to the west, Goa to the northwest, Maharashtra to the north, Telangana to the northeast, Andhra Pradesh to the east, Tamil Nadu to the southeast, and Kerala to the south. It is the only southern state to have land borders with all of the other 4 southern Indian sister states.

Places to Visit

Places to Visit in Karnataka

Lalbagh Botanical Garden

Lalbagh is a 240 acres (0.97 km2) garden and is located in south Bengaluru. It holds two flower shows and has over 1,000 species of plants with many trees that are more than a hundred years old.

ISKCON Temple Bangalore

ISKCON Temple Bangalore is one of the largest Krishna Hindu temples in the world, affiliated with ISKCON Revival Movement. It is located at Rajajinagar, in North Bangalore.

Bangalore Palace

Bangalore Palace is a royal residence located in Bengaluru that is owned by the Wadiyar Dynasty and is open to tourists and public. This palace was a summer resting place for the Wadiyars.

Statue of Lord Shiva

The Statue of Lord Shiva in Murudeshwar is the second biggest statue of Lord Shiva in the world. The statue and temple are located on the beach in Murudeshwara, which lies on the Arabian Sea coastal line.

Jog Falls

It is the second highest plunge waterfall in India. It is a segmented waterfall which depends on rain and season becomes a plunge waterfall.

Sree Virupaksha Temple, Hampi

Virupaksha Temple is located in Hampi in the Ballari district of Karnataka, India. It is part of the Group of Monuments at Hampi, designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Mysore Palace

The Mysore Palace is a historical palace and the royal residence at Mysore in the Indian state of Karnataka. It is the official residence of the Wadiyar dynasty and the seat of the Kingdom of Mysore.

Brindavan Gardens

The Bringavan Garden is laid out in 3 terraces which contain water fountains, Ficus trees, foliage plants such as Duranta plumaria and Euphorbia and flowering plants like Celosia, Marigold and bougainvillea.

Bandipur Tiger Reserve

The Bandipur Tiger Reserve is a forested area with tigers & safaris. There are lots of animals found in this reserve including elephants, tigers and deer.
